\begin{itemize}
\item $f(3) = 5$ and $f(5) = 0$.
\item $f'(3) = f'(5) = 0$.
\item $f'(x)>0$ if $x<3$ or $x>5$.
\item $f'(x)<0$ if $3<x<5$.
\end{itemize}

		\begin{solution}[5cm]
There are a bunch of solutions.  One important piece of information is that $x=3$ should be a local max and $x=5$ is a local min.

		\question Let $f(x) = x^3+2x^2+x$.  
		\begin{parts}
			\part Determine where $f'(x)>0$ and where $f'(x)<0$ (Hint: factor $f'(x)$). Use this information to determine where $f(x)$ is increasing and decreasing.  Determine any local max/min values. Then sketch the function. 
		\begin{solution}[5cm]
    $f'(x) = 3x^2+4x+1 = (3x+1)(x+1)$.  Therefore, $f'(x) = 0$ when $x= -1,\frac{-1}{3}$. Plugging in $x$-values of $-2,\frac{-2}{3}, 1$ tell us that $f'>0$ on $(-\infty,-1) $ and $(\frac{-1}{3},\infty)$ and $f'<0$ on $(-1,\frac{-1}{3})$.
    
    Therefore, $f$ has a local max value of $0$ at $x=-1$ and $f$ has a local min value of $\frac{-4}{27}$ at $x=\frac{-1}{3}$.
		\end{solution}
